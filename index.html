Semester Reflection

What I learned: Throughout this semester so far, I learned how to build projects that are both creative and usable. I developed a variety of skills that I did not have prior to this class, including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Tableau, mapping, and working with CSV and Python scripts. Through these labs, I have also learned how to create projects for real people and the importance of making their experience positive and simple. That meant making choices that would improve the clarity and usability, like simplifying what the viewer sees, keeping a consistent style, and designing for different screens and zoom levels. I also learned how much trust is shaped by presentation. Lab 2 showed me that AI can sound confident even when it shouldn’t, Lab 3 showed me that a visualization can look professional while leaving out key context, and Lab 4 taught me that data can create strong stories even when the definitions or baselines are unclear. After these labs, my goal wasn’t just to have an aesthetically pleasing portfolio website, but to pay attention to how I was perceived by data and think about how my design affects the way viewers think.

Challenges I overcame: The biggest challenge I had to overcome was being in a class with peers with a lot of coding background, while I had very little. It was very difficult for me to learn new languages almost every week while still trying to ensure my portfolio website was functional and clean. I had a lot of moments where such a small detail would break everything, like file paths, formatting issues, or weird encoding problems. I had to get used to troubleshooting as part of the process instead of taking it like I was doing all of it wrong. I worked through it by using my resources and not staying stuck. I tested things step by step, compared my work to examples, used AI support when I needed help debugging, and then went back through to make sure I understood what was happening instead of just accepting the fix. Over time, I got more confident working through problems even when I didn’t immediately know what the answer was.

How I grew: I feel like my understanding of digital culture and data analytics grew more and more from each lab. In Lab 2, I quickly learned how AI tools try to frame what success looks like and how easily they can sound correct even when they shouldn’t. Lab 3 helped me realize the same thing applies to visualizations. A chart can look clean and convincing and still leave out context or push people toward the wrong conclusion. Then Lab 4 through Lab 6 helped me connect that to my own work, because I had to decide what to show, what to simplify, and how to design things so people can actually understand them. I also learned new ways to use my creativity through websites and mapping, where I can make something aesthetic but still functional and clear.

Looking ahead: Looking ahead, I want to use these skills to make things that actually help others in the real world. I can see myself using what I learned in data analytics to compare data, find patterns, and present it in a way that makes sense instead of overwhelming people. I also want to keep building little tools that simplify my workflow, like a website that organizes information for a project or something that automates repetitive tasks. I know I’ll keep using AI, but I hope to continue using it to brainstorm and help elaborate on ideas I already had. My goal is to keep building skills until I don’t need as much help, and I can create more of these features on my own.
